Current order list

The order list starts empty. Click "Generate list" to auto-fill it with items that are low or out of stock — each one comes with a recommended box count based on how it's been selling. Use the search above to add any other items manually. Items are grouped by category. (Esh orders have their own dedicated page — the "Esh Order" tab — where they're split across delivery days.)

Checkboxes let you hold things back without losing them. Uncheck an individual item (or a whole category using the checkbox in its header) to keep it on the list but leave it out of this week's order. When you click "Mark as ordered," only the checked items go to Order arrivals — anything unchecked stays right here for next time. The ✕ button removes an item from the list entirely.

Use the category dropdown in the toolbar when you need to focus on one supplier (like ice cream from a separate vendor).

🌽 Esh Order

This builds your full weekly Esh order, then splits it across your delivery days. Click "Generate Esh order" to fill it with Esh items that need restocking (only items with an Esh product code). Set your delivery days and how each category divides between them under "Delivery split settings." The preview below shows how the order breaks out per day. When you click "Place split order," each day becomes its own pending order on the Order arrivals page, named like "Wednesday Esh Order."

To fine-tune a single item (say, send all the cinnamon rolls on Friday only), expand its category below and set that item's own split.

Box sizes

Set how many individual units come in one box for each item (e.g. 12 if a case has 12 bags). This is saved permanently until you change it. Box sizes are used to suggest accurate box counts in the order list and Suggested reorder column.

Orders you've placed appear here as "Pending arrival" (yellow border). When a delivery shows up, you have two ways to confirm it. "Confirm with Esh PDF" lets you upload the Esh invoice PDF directly — the website reads it on your device, matches each item by its Clover product code, fills in how many cases arrived, and flags anything short, missing, extra, or backordered. (Reading the PDF can take a minute or two for a long invoice.) "Confirm manually" lets you check items off by hand. Either way, your stock updates automatically. After an order is received you can click "Open" to undo it or copy it back to your order list to reorder.

The Clover import file isn't downloaded automatically — when you're ready to sync to Clover, click the yellow "↓ Download Clover file" button on the most recent received order (marked with a ★ "Latest · most accurate" badge). That file has your latest stock counts and is the right one to upload to Clover. Older received orders keep download buttons too in case you ever need to re-grab one, but they're labeled "older" since their counts are outdated.

Received orders stay here as a permanent history (green border) until you click "Remove" on them. Esh automatically rolls any backordered items into your next order, so there's nothing extra to track here.

🐌 Slow movers

Selling fast highlights items moving well above their normal pace — items selling much more than usual, where you may want to order extra so you don't run out. Slow movers surfaces items barely selling, so you can spot dead stock before reordering, discount it, or discontinue it. Everything is measured over the date window you pick (comparing it against the prior window of equal length), so the trends reflect calendar time, not how often you upload.

📅 Seasonal sales history

Upload your Clover Item Sales reports here — one per month works best. The website reads each month's sales per item and builds a year-round picture, so it can see busy stretches (like the Fourth of July) and slow ones (after August). Once it has last year's months, it flags upcoming busy periods and scales your suggested reorder amounts up or down to match how things actually sold last year.

🎉 Big days

Mark dates that need extra stock — the Fourth of July, Labor Day weekend, etc. When a big day falls within the reorder window, suggested order amounts get boosted. You can set a single date or a weekend span, choose a boost level or type your own, and have it repeat every year.

In Clover: Reports → Item and Service sales. Set the date range to a single month, then export to CSV. Repeat for each month you want history for (a year gives the best picture). Upload them all here — you can add more anytime, and re-uploading a month just updates it.

Sync check

Catches potential data-entry errors and drift between Clover and this site. Items get flagged if their stock changed in ways that don't match what was sold + what was restocked, if sales spiked far above normal, or if more was sold than was actually on the shelf. Review each flagged item to see if Clover got an incorrect adjustment.

Wednesday discard

Items here are perishable goods (baked goods, etc.) that spoil and get thrown out. Add the items you regularly discard. Click "Discard all now" on Wednesday morning when you toss the old stock — it sets every listed item's count to zero so your inventory reflects the fresh start.

This list also auto-zeros every Tuesday at 11:59 PM, so even if you forget to click the button, the counts reset before Wednesday's fresh delivery. The items stay on this list permanently — only their stock gets zeroed.

When a brand-new item shows up in a Clover export, the website will ask whether you want to add it to this discard list. Items are never added automatically.

How this works

This site is shared. Everyone with the link sees and edits the same live data — no separate copies, no spreadsheet uploads needed for viewers.

The weekly routine:

  1. In Clover, export your inventory (the multi-tab Excel file).
  2. Click "Upload export." It updates stock for everyone, picks up any new items you added in Clover, and compares to last week.
  3. Check the weekly summary, then the Order list tab to see what to buy.

Box sizes: visit the "Box sizes" tab to set how many individual units come in one box for each item (e.g. 12 if a case holds 12 bags). This saves permanently and powers all the box-based suggestions. You can also set the box size in the "+ Add" restock popup.

Smarter reorder suggestions: the suggested box count adapts to how each item is selling. Items selling fast get bigger reorders; slow movers get smaller ones. Always rounded up to whole boxes.

Logging deliveries: when stock arrives, the cleanest path is the Order list → Order arrivals workflow described below. For a one-off "I just got more of this one item" situation, click "＋ Add" on that row to enter boxes, units per box, and loose units.

Quick Clover download: the "↓ Download Clover file" button in the header at any point downloads a Clover-ready import spreadsheet reflecting the current state of your stock. Useful any time Clover and the website have drifted out of sync.

Order list & arrivals: items below their alert threshold show up automatically in the Order list tab. Add others manually with the search. When you actually place the order, hit "Mark as ordered" — it goes to the Order arrivals tab as a pending arrival and the order list clears for next time.

Backups: data lives in cloud storage and is reliable, but "Download backup" gives you a safety file you can keep in Google Drive just in case.

Store hours (used by the upload warning)

These hours control when the "data may be outdated" warning appears at the top of the page. Set the open and close time for each day. Check the box to mark a day as closed.
